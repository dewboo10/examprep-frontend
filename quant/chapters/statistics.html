Introduction to Statistics

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data. It helps us make sense of numerical information and draw meaningful conclusions.

Key Concepts

  • Population: The entire group being studied
  • Sample: A subset of the population
  • Variable: A characteristic that can take different values
  • Data: The values of variables collected for analysis

Basic Statistical Terms

  • Frequency: Number of times a value occurs
  • Relative Frequency: Frequency divided by total observations
  • Cumulative Frequency: Sum of frequencies up to a point
  • Class Interval: Range of values in grouped data

Measures of Central Tendency

Mean (Arithmetic Average)

For ungrouped data:

Mean = Σx/n

For grouped data:

Mean = Σ(fx)/Σf

Median

For ungrouped data:

Median = (n+1)/2 th observation

For grouped data:

Median = L + (n/2 - cf)/f × h

Mode

For ungrouped data:

Mode = Most frequent value

For grouped data:

Mode = L + (f₁ - f₀)/(2f₁ - f₀ - f₂) × h

Measures of Dispersion

Range

Range = Maximum Value - Minimum Value

Variance

For ungrouped data:

σ² = Σ(x - μ)²/n

For grouped data:

σ² = Σf(x - μ)²/Σf

Standard Deviation

σ = √Variance

Probability

Basic Probability

P(A) = n(A)/n(S)

where:

  • P(A) is the probability of event A
  • n(A) is the number of favorable outcomes
  • n(S) is the total number of possible outcomes

Addition Rule

P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Multiplication Rule

P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B|A)

Probability Distributions

Binomial Distribution

P(X = r) = nCr × p^r × (1-p)^(n-r)

where:

  • n is the number of trials
  • r is the number of successes
  • p is the probability of success

Normal Distribution

f(x) = (1/σ√2π) × e^(-(x-μ)²/2σ²)

where:

  • μ is the mean
  • σ is the standard deviation

Correlation and Regression

Correlation Coefficient

r = Σ((x-μx)(y-μy))/(nσxσy)

Regression Line

y = mx + b

where:

  • m = r(σy/σx)
  • b = μy - mμx

Practice Questions

Measures of Central Tendency

Question 1: Arithmetic Mean
Find the arithmetic mean of the following data set: 12, 15, 18, 20, 25, 30, 35
Solution:

Mean = (12 + 15 + 18 + 20 + 25 + 30 + 35)/7

= 155/7 = 22.14

Question 2: Weighted Mean
Calculate the weighted mean of the following data:
Value Weight
80 2
85 3
90 4
Solution:

Weighted Mean = (80×2 + 85×3 + 90×4)/(2 + 3 + 4)

= (160 + 255 + 360)/9 = 775/9 = 86.11

Question 3: Geometric Mean
Find the geometric mean of the numbers: 2, 4, 8, 16
Solution:

Geometric Mean = (2 × 4 × 8 × 16)^(1/4)

= (1024)^(1/4) = 5.66

Question 4: Combined Mean
The mean of 20 numbers is 15. If 5 more numbers with mean 20 are added, find the new mean.
Solution:

Total of first 20 numbers = 20 × 15 = 300

Total of 5 new numbers = 5 × 20 = 100

New mean = (300 + 100)/25 = 16

Question 1: Simple Median
Find the median of: 7, 3, 9, 5, 11, 2, 8
Solution:

Arranged order: 2, 3, 5, 7, 8, 9, 11

Median = 7 (middle value)

Question 2: Even Number of Terms
Find the median of: 12, 15, 18, 20, 25, 30
Solution:

Median = (18 + 20)/2 = 19

Question 3: Grouped Data Median
Find the median for the following grouped data:
Class Interval Frequency
0-10 5
10-20 8
20-30 12
30-40 7
Solution:

Total frequency = 32

Median class = 20-30

Median = 20 + (16-13)/12 × 10 = 22.5

Question 4: Missing Value
The median of 5 numbers is 8. If one number is removed, the median becomes 7. Find the removed number.
Solution:

Original numbers: a, b, 8, c, d

After removal: a, b, c, d

Median = (b + c)/2 = 7

Therefore, b + c = 14

Since 8 was the median, it must be the removed number

Question 1: Simple Mode
Find the mode of: 3, 5, 7, 5, 9, 5, 11, 7
Solution:

Mode = 5 (appears most frequently)

Question 2: Bimodal Data
Find the mode of: 2, 3, 4, 4, 5, 6, 6, 7
Solution:

Modes = 4 and 6 (both appear twice)

Question 3: Grouped Data Mode
Find the mode for the following grouped data:
Class Interval Frequency
0-10 5
10-20 12
20-30 8
30-40 3
Solution:

Modal class = 10-20

Mode = 10 + (12-5)/(2×12-5-8) × 10 = 16.36

Question 4: Missing Frequency
In a frequency distribution, the mode is 25 and the frequency of the modal class is 15. If the frequency of the class preceding the modal class is 8, find the frequency of the class succeeding the modal class.
Solution:

Let f₂ be the required frequency

Mode = 25 = L + (15-8)/(2×15-8-f₂) × h

Solving: f₂ = 10

Measures of Dispersion

Question 1: Simple Range
Find the range of: 5, 8, 12, 15, 20, 25
Solution:

Range = Maximum - Minimum

= 25 - 5 = 20

Question 2: Coefficient of Range
Find the coefficient of range for the data: 10, 15, 20, 25, 30
Solution:

Range = 30 - 10 = 20

Coefficient of Range = (30-10)/(30+10) = 0.5

Question 3: Range with Outliers
Find the range of: 2, 5, 7, 8, 9, 50
Solution:

Range = 50 - 2 = 48

Note: The value 50 is an outlier

Question 4: Range in Grouped Data
Find the range for the following grouped data:
Class Interval Frequency
0-10 5
10-20 8
20-30 12
Solution:

Range = Upper limit of highest class - Lower limit of lowest class

= 30 - 0 = 30

Question 1: Simple Standard Deviation
Find the standard deviation of: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10
Solution:

Mean = 6

Variance = ((2-6)² + (4-6)² + (6-6)² + (8-6)² + (10-6)²)/5 = 8

Standard Deviation = √8 = 2.83

Question 2: Coefficient of Variation
Find the coefficient of variation for the data: 10, 15, 20, 25, 30
Solution:

Mean = 20

Standard Deviation = 7.07

Coefficient of Variation = (7.07/20) × 100 = 35.35%

Question 3: Combined Standard Deviation
Two groups have means 20 and 30, standard deviations 5 and 6, and sizes 50 and 40 respectively. Find the combined standard deviation.
Solution:

Combined mean = (20×50 + 30×40)/90 = 24.44

Combined variance = ((50×25 + 40×36) + (50×4.44² + 40×5.56²))/90 = 30.44

Combined SD = √30.44 = 5.52

Question 4: Standard Deviation with Frequency
Find the standard deviation for the following data:
Value Frequency
5 2
10 3
15 4
Solution:

Mean = (5×2 + 10×3 + 15×4)/9 = 11.11

Variance = (2×(5-11.11)² + 3×(10-11.11)² + 4×(15-11.11)²)/9 = 15.43

Standard Deviation = √15.43 = 3.93

Probability

Question 1: Simple Probability
A bag contains 5 red, 3 blue, and 2 green balls. What is the probability of drawing a red ball?
Solution:

Total balls = 10

Red balls = 5

Probability = 5/10 = 0.5

Question 2: Compound Probability
Two dice are rolled. What is the probability of getting a sum of 7?
Solution:

Favorable outcomes: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1)

Total outcomes = 36

Probability = 6/36 = 1/6

Question 3: Conditional Probability
In a class of 30 students, 20 are boys and 10 are girls. 5 boys and 3 girls wear glasses. What is the probability that a student wears glasses given that they are a boy?
Solution:

P(Glasses|Boy) = 5/20 = 0.25

Question 4: Independent Events
A coin is tossed twice. What is the probability of getting heads both times?
Solution:

P(First head) = 1/2

P(Second head) = 1/2

P(Both heads) = (1/2) × (1/2) = 1/4

Probability Distributions

Question 1: Binomial Probability
A fair coin is tossed 5 times. What is the probability of getting exactly 3 heads?
Solution:

n = 5, r = 3, p = 0.5

P(X=3) = 5C3 × (0.5)³ × (0.5)² = 0.3125

Question 2: Binomial Mean and Variance
For a binomial distribution with n=10 and p=0.3, find the mean and variance.
Solution:

Mean = np = 10 × 0.3 = 3

Variance = npq = 10 × 0.3 × 0.7 = 2.1

Question 3: Normal Distribution
For a normal distribution with mean 50 and standard deviation 10, find the probability that X is between 40 and 60.
Solution:

Z₁ = (40-50)/10 = -1

Z₂ = (60-50)/10 = 1

P(-1 < Z < 1) = 0.6826

Question 4: Normal Approximation
A fair coin is tossed 100 times. Using normal approximation, find the probability of getting between 45 and 55 heads.
Solution:

Mean = np = 50

SD = √(npq) = 5

P(45 < X < 55) = P(-1 < Z < 1) = 0.6826
