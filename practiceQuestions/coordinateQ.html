‚ñ∂1. Find the distance between points (3, 4) and (7, 7).
Explanation:
To find the distance between two points in the plane, use the distance formula:
Distance = ‚àö[(x‚ÇÇ - x‚ÇÅ)¬≤ + (y‚ÇÇ - y‚ÇÅ)¬≤]
Here, (x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ) = (3, 4) and (x‚ÇÇ, y‚ÇÇ) = (7, 7).
Calculate the differences: x‚ÇÇ - x‚ÇÅ = 7 - 3 = 4, y‚ÇÇ - y‚ÇÅ = 7 - 4 = 3.
Now square and add: 4¬≤ + 3¬≤ = 16 + 9 = 25.

Answer: 5 units.
‚ñ∂2. Find the midpoint of the line segment joining (2, 3) and (4, 5).
Explanation:

Midpoint = ((x‚ÇÅ + x‚ÇÇ)/2, (y‚ÇÅ + y‚ÇÇ)/2)

‚ñ∂3. Find the slope of the line passing through (1, 2) and (3, 6).
Explanation:
The slope (m) of a line through two points is:
m = (y‚ÇÇ - y‚ÇÅ) / (x‚ÇÇ - x‚ÇÅ)
Here, (x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ) = (1, 2), (x‚ÇÇ, y‚ÇÇ) = (3, 6).

‚ñ∂4. If the slope of the line joining (2, y) and (4, 5) is 2, find y.
