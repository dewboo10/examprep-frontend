▶1. 60% of a number is equal to 3/5 of another number. If the first number is 150, what is the second number?
Answer: 150
Explanation:
- Step 1: 60% of the first number means 60/100 × 150 = 90.
- Step 2: Let the second number be x. 3/5 of x = 90.
- Step 3: To find x, set up the equation: (3/5)x = 90.
- Step 4: Multiply both sides by 5: 3x = 450.
- Step 5: Divide both sides by 3: x = 150.
- Conclusion: The second number is 150.

▶3. A number is first increased by 30% and then decreased by 30%. What is the net percentage change?
Answer: 9% decrease
Explanation:
- Step 1: Let the original number be 100.
- Step 2: Increase by 30%: 100 + 30 = 130.
- Step 3: Decrease 130 by 30%: 130 - (30% of 130) = 130 - 39 = 91.
- Step 4: Net change = Final value - Original value = 91 - 100 = -9.
- Step 5: Net percentage change = (-9 / 100) × 100 = -9%.
- Formula used: Net % change = a + b + (ab/100), where a = 30, b = -30.
- Conclusion: The net change is a 9% decrease.
