▶1. 60% of a number is equal to 3/5 of another number. If the first number is 150, what is the second number?
Answer: 150
Explanation:
- Step 1: 60% of the first number means 60/100 × 150 = 90.
- Step 2: Let the second number be x. 3/5 of x = 90.
- Step 3: To find x, set up the equation: (3/5)x = 90.
- Step 4: Multiply both sides by 5: 3x = 450.
- Step 5: Divide both sides by 3: x = 150.
- Conclusion: The second number is 150.
▶2. If A is 25% more than B, then B is what percent less than A?
Answer: 20%
Explanation:
- Step 1: Let B = 100 (choose a simple value for easier calculation).
- Step 2: A is 25% more than B, so A = 100 + 25 = 125.
- Step 3: The difference between A and B is 25.
- Step 4: To find what percent B is less than A, use the formula:
    Percent less = (Difference / A) × 100 = (25 / 125) × 100 = 20%.
- Conclusion: B is 20% less than A.
▶3. A number is first increased by 30% and then decreased by 30%. What is the net percentage change?
Answer: 9% decrease
Explanation:
- Step 1: Let the original number be 100.
- Step 2: Increase by 30%: 100 + 30 = 130.
- Step 3: Decrease 130 by 30%: 130 - (30% of 130) = 130 - 39 = 91.
- Step 4: Net change = Final value - Original value = 91 - 100 = -9.
- Step 5: Net percentage change = (-9 / 100) × 100 = -9%.
- Formula used: Net % change = a + b + (ab/100), where a = 30, b = -30.
- Conclusion: The net change is a 9% decrease.

▶A company's profit increases by x% in 2021, decreases by (x+10)% in 2022, and increases by (x+20)% in 2023. If the final profit equals the initial profit, find x.
Answer: 20%
Explanation:
- Step 1: Let the initial profit be 100 units.
- Step 2: After a x% increase in 2021: 100 × (1 + x/100) = 100 + x.
- Step 3: After a (x+10)% decrease in 2022: (100 + x) × (1 - (x+10)/100).
- Step 4: After a (x+20)% increase in 2023: Result × (1 + (x+20)/100).
- Step 5: Set the final profit equal to the initial profit (100) and solve for x:
    100 × (1 + x/100) × (1 - (x+10)/100) × (1 + (x+20)/100) = 100
- Step 6: Simplify and solve: x = 20.
- Conclusion: x = 20%.

▶A shopkeeper marks up an item by x% and offers a discount of x%. A customer buys the item using a 10% cashback coupon. If the final price is ₹891, what was the original price?
Answer: ₹1000
Explanation:
- Step 1: Let the original price be P.
- Step 2: After markup: P × (1 + x/100).
- Step 3: After x% discount: P × (1 + x/100) × (1 - x/100) = P × (1 - x²/10000).
- Step 4: After 10% cashback: Final price = P × (1 - x²/10000) × 0.9.
- Step 5: Set equal to 891: P × (1 - x²/10000) × 0.9 = 891.
- Step 6: Given x = 10, substitute and solve: P × (1 - 0.01) × 0.9 = 891 → P × 0.99 × 0.9 = 891 → P = 1000.
- Conclusion: The original price was ₹1000.

▶Arjun Kapoor and Anil Kapoor appear for a test. For each correct answer, 1 mark is awarded and for each wrong answer, 1/2 mark is deducted. Arjun answers some questions and gets 10% wrong, securing a score of 85% which is 6 marks more than the pass marks. Anil gets 20% wrong, scores 70% which is 3 marks less than the pass mark. No marks for unanswered questions. What is the pass mark?
A. 64   B. 51   C. 45   D. 25   E. None of these
Answer: 45
Explanation:
Let Arjun attempt x questions, 10% wrong.
Score = 0.9x - 0.1x × 1 = 0.85x
0.85x = 0.85z (z = total marks possible) ⇒ x = z
Similarly, Anil attempts y questions, 20% wrong.
Score = 0.8y - 0.2y × 1 = 0.7y
0.7y = 0.7z ⇒ y = z
0.85z = P + 6 (P = pass mark)
0.7z = P - 3
0.15z = 9 ⇒ z = 60
0.7 × 60 = P - 3 ⇒ P = 45
Correct Option: C
