Bharat Ratna
Bharat Ratna is India's highest civilian award.
Instituted
It was instituted in 1954.
Purpose
Awarded for exceptional service in art, literature, science, and public service.
Eligibility
It is not limited to Indian citizens (e.g., Nelson Mandela).
Monetary Grant
Award carries no monetary grant, only a certificate and medallion.
First Recipients
First Bharat Ratna recipients (1954): C. Rajagopalachari, Dr. Radhakrishnan, C.V. Raman.
Total Recipients
As of 2023, 48 individuals have received the Bharat Ratna.
Last Awarded
Last awarded in 2019 to Pranab Mukherjee, Nanaji Deshmukh, Bhupen Hazarika.
Padma Awards
Padma Awards are given in 3 categories: Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan, Padma Shri.
Padma Vibhushan
Padma Vibhushan is the second-highest civilian award.
Padma Vibhushan Instituted
Instituted in 1954; awarded for exceptional service.
Padma Bhushan
Padma Bhushan is the third-highest.
Padma Shri
Padma Shri is the fourth-highest civilian honor.
Announcement
Announced every year on Republic Day (26 January).
2024 Padma Awards
In 2024, 132 people received Padma awards.
Posthumous Awards
Awards can be given posthumously.
Dadasaheb Phalke
Dadasaheb Phalke received Bharat Ratna in 1969 (100 years after birth).
Famous Recipients
Lata Mangeshkar, Sachin Tendulkar, Atal Bihari Vajpayee are Bharat Ratna awardees.
Politicians
Maximum Bharat Ratna awards have gone to politicians.
Titles
Civilian awards cannot be used as titles.

Gallantry Awards

Param Vir Chakra
Highest military decoration. For acts of valour in presence of enemy. First recipient: Major Somnath Sharma (1947). Latest: Captain Vikram Batra (1999, posthumous).
Maha Vir Chakra
Second highest military decoration. For acts of conspicuous gallantry. 2023 recipient: Colonel Bikumalla Santosh Babu (posthumous).
Vir Chakra
Third highest military decoration. For acts of gallantry in presence of enemy. 2023 recipients: Major Anil Urs, Havildar Alok Kumar Dubey.
Ashoka Chakra
Highest peacetime gallantry award. For most conspicuous bravery. 2023 recipient: Major Anil Urs (posthumous).
